Beginning of Animations

18-07-2024

A lengthy post of alot of progress. Later in the post, I import the player character and use a shader to layer 2d clothing textures to achieve a flat effect.

21-06

On this day, I implemented the player's ability to pick up and drop objects, here are following clips of the mechanisms I implemented.

^ Beginnings of picking up, using a visible target that the object moves to.

^ Now have added the ability to pick up backpacks and equip them, you can still pick them up like any other object, you equip and unequip backpacks using F. There is a time delay picking and placing them- this is to increase difficulty- I plan to increase this time delay in numerous difficulty settings for my game- for example, a longer time is needed to take off and put on a backpack if higher difficulty.


Afterwards, I decided to create a potential nemesis featured in the scarier part of the game, during the ritual, Satan himself.

24-06

02/03-07

These two days, I 3d modelled a Shadow Person and the Hatman. I want these entities to show up when the player is very fatigued and anxious in scarier stages of the game. I'm unsure which shadow person variant to include as of yet, as the faded one is very on theme with the Hatman.

Afterwards, I finally imported the player model I have been working on since 2023.

To conclude this post, I added animations using Unity's scrollwheel while moving to increase or decrease speed. This mechanic cycles through walking -> jogging -> running -> sprinting depending on how far the player has scrolled upwards or downwards using their scrollwheel. Animations are managed by Unity's Animator and Animator Controller, which made the process MUCH easier than I had imagined, as I had been putting off importing the player for a long time due to the fear that animation and texturing was going to be very difficult.
